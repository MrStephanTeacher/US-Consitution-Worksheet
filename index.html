Task 3 — Inferencing & analysis · ~8 min
3 Answer the following questions in 2–4 sentences. Use evidence from the text. critical thinking

3a. Why do you think the Framers separated legislative, executive, and judicial power into three distinct branches rather than concentrating it in one body?

3b. Article I, Section 9 lists things Congress is forbidden to do. What does this tell us about the Framers' priorities? Give at least one specific example from the text.

3c. The Preamble uses phrases like "domestic Tranquility" and "general Welfare". In your own words, what vision of government do these phrases suggest?

The US Constitution at a Glance
Your visual summary — the three branches and their powers

L Legislative Branch

Article I · Congress
  • House of Representatives & Senate
  • Makes laws
  • Levies taxes & controls spending
  • Declares war
  • Sole power of impeachment (House)
  • Tries impeachment cases (Senate)
Rep: min. 25 yrs  ·  Senator: min. 30 yrs

E Executive Branch

Article II · President
  • Carries out laws
  • Commander in Chief
  • Signs or vetoes bills
  • Appoints judges & ministers
  • Negotiates treaties
  • Gives State of the Union address
Min. 35 yrs  ·  natural-born citizen  ·  4-year term

J Judicial Branch

Article III · Supreme Court
  • Interprets the law
  • Hears constitutional cases
  • Original jurisdiction over states
  • Trial by jury guaranteed
  • Judges serve during "good behaviour"
  • Defines & punishes treason
Appointed by President, confirmed by Senate
Checks & Balances — how each branch limits the others
Preamble goals:   form a more perfect Union  ·  establish Justice  ·  ensure domestic Tranquility  ·  provide for common defence  ·  promote general Welfare  ·  secure the Blessings of Liberty
